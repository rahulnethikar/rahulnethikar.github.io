## BUSINESS CASE
The Verizon DSL Technical Support queue at Mahindra Satyam BPO is currently experiencing Service Level (SL) performance below the contractual requirement of 85%. High Average Handle Time (AHT) and a surge in repeat calls have led to increased customer wait times (>30 seconds) and call drop rates. Furthermore, high outbound callback utilization is draining frontline availability. This project aims to stabilize operations center-wide by optimizing AHT, enhancing First Call Resolution (FCR), and implementing a structured coaching governance model.

## PROBLEM STATEMENT
Since Q1 2010, the Verizon DSL Technical Support center-wide Service Level has averaged 78%, falling short of the 85% SLA. Customers are experiencing wait times exceeding 45 seconds, and the call drop rate has spiked to 7%. The current AHT is 7.4 minutes, against a target of 6.2 minutes, primarily due to inconsistent troubleshooting, repeat calls (22%), and excessive reliance on outbound callbacks (18% utilization).

## GOAL STATEMENT
To improve the center-wide Service Level from 78% to ≥90% by end of Q3 2010. 
**Sub-targets:**
*   Reduce Average Wait Time from 45s to < 20s.
*   Decrease Call Drop Rate from 7% to < 5%.
*   Reduce AHT from 7.4 min to ≤ 6.2 min.
*   Decrease Outbound Utilization from 18% to < 10%.
*   Improve FCR from 78% to > 88%.

## SCOPE
*   **In-Scope:** All incoming and outgoing technical support call flows, coaching processes, floor governance, and SME escalation workflows center-wide.
*   **Out-of-Scope:** Payroll, facility management, and client-side server infrastructure.
