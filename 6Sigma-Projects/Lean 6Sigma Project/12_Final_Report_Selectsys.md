# FINAL PROJECT REPORT
**Project:** Improving CSAT, Agent Utilization, and Quality
**Company:** Selectsys India
**Client:** US Tech Support
**Timeline:** Feb 2012 – May 2012

---

## 1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
The US Tech Support project was initiated to resolve critical deficiencies in customer experience and operational efficiency. The project followed a 3-month Lean-driven DMAIC framework led by Rahul Nethikar (AM) under the guidance of the Operations Manager.

## 2. ACHIEVEMENTS VS. GOALS
| **Metric** | **Baseline (Feb)** | **Target** | **Final (May)** | **Outcome** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CSAT** | 75% | 95% | **95.2%** | ✅ Achieved |
| **Utilization** | 30% | 90% | **90.3%** | ✅ Achieved |
| **Reach Rate** | 30% | 70% | **76.3%** | ✅ Achieved |
| **QA Score** | Low | 90% | **91.3%** | ✅ Achieved |

## 3. CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS
*   **The "Restart Disclaimer":** Fixing the LMI disconnect journey was the single largest contributor to the 20% jump in CSAT.
*   **Real-time Governance:** Daily 2-hourly utilization reports enabled immediate correction of idle behavior.
*   **Standardized Communication:** The preformatted library professionalized the Chat interaction, leading to higher perceived service value.
*   **Discipline Enforcement:** Synergizing Biometrics with VOIP login status significantly reduced absenteeism and tardiness.

## 4. CONCLUSION
The project has successfully transitioned from a high-leakage, low-satisfaction operation to a high-performance center. Rahul Nethikar and the respective Team Leaders (Chat/Voice) have established a robust Control Plan to sustain these results.
