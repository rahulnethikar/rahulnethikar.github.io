## BUSINESS CASE
The Verizon DSL Technical Support center at Mahindra Satyam BPO is currently struggling with a Net Promoter Score (NPS) of ~15%, significantly trailing the target of 30%. Low NPS indicates high customer dissatisfaction, which impacts contract stability and brand loyalty. Preliminary analysis suggests that inconsistent troubleshooting, poor empathy, and high repeat call rates are primary drivers of detractors. By leveraging a comprehensive Time & Motion Study, this project will identify granular process inefficiencies and behavioral gaps to drive NPS to 25–30%.

## PROBLEM STATEMENT
As of Early 2011, the Centre NPS for Verizon DSL is ~15%, which is 50% below the target of 30%. Analysis of detractor comments highlights frustration with long hold times, repetitive questioning, and lack of technical resolution. The current FCR is ~68%, and AHT is 6.5 minutes, indicating that speed is being prioritized over quality, leading to poor customer perception.

## GOAL STATEMENT
Improve Centre NPS from ~15% to 25–30% within 3 months (by April 2011).
**Sub-targets:**
*   Reduce Repeat Call Rate from 32% to < 22%.
*   Improve FCR from 68% to > 75%.
*   Optimize AHT from 6.5 min to 6.2 min without compromising resolution.
*   Improve QA Score from 82% to > 90%.

## SCOPE
*   **In-Scope:** All inbound voice operations for Verizon DSL Technical Support center-wide. Time & Motion Study of 120 calls across all shifts and issue categories.
*   **Out-of-Scope:** Payroll, billing systems (external), and client-side hardware manufacturing.
