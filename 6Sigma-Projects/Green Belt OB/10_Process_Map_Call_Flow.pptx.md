## SLIDE 2: End-to-End Call Flow (Workgroup Level)
1. **CALL ARRIVAL:** Customer enters Verizon DSL queue (Filtered for Jaydeep's Floor).
2. **AUTHENTICATION:** Agent verifies customer and service location.
3. **DIAGNOSIS (LO):** Basic checks (power, cables).
4. **DIAGNOSIS (L1):** Advanced troubleshooting (Isolation, Filters, Modem Reset).
5. **DECISION:** Is the issue fixed? 
   - YES: Close Ticket.
   - NO: Determine if Outbound/Callback is needed for follow-up testing.
6. **OUTBOUND INITIATION:** Agent puts customer on hold or hangs up to call back later.

---

## SLIDE 3: Where Outbound is Triggered
*   **Merchantile Line Test (MLT):** Agent starts a line test, sees it will take a few minutes, and tells the customer "I'll call you back with the result" just to clear the call. This is our biggest pain point.
*   **Call Drop:** Lines are unstable; agent dials back to ensure the customer isn't left hanging.
*   **AHT Management:** Agents with long calls dial out at the 10-minute mark to "park" the issue and reset their talk-timer.
*   **WFM Latency:** Waiting for a back-office specialist to look at a port.

---

## SLIDE 4: Floor Pain Points
1. **Agent Discretion:** Right now, there's no lock on the phones; anyone can dial out for any reason.
2. **Lazy Troubleshooting:** Agents are offering a callback for MLT results instead of performing "Dead Air" holds or multitasking.
3. **Shift Transitions:** The floor is ghosting inbound calls in the last 20 minutes of their shift by being "stuck" on outbound follow-ups.
4. **Escalation Gaps:** Complex technical issues are just being bounced back and forth instead of going to the SIG specialized unit.
