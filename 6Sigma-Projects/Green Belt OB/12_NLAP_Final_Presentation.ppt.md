## SLIDE 2: Top Causes of Outbound (Pareto Analysis)
Analysis of the typical 21% volume reveals three main categories:
1.  **MLT Valid Callbacks (8%):** Essential technical follow-ups. **Not for reduction.**
2.  **Unnecessary "Check-In" Calls (7%):** Agents calling back just to "see if it's still good" with no technical change.
3.  **AHT Management/Parking (6%):** Dialing out at the end of a shift or to reset a long call timer.

*Note: The 80/20 focus is on Categories 2 and 3, which account for 13% of the total 21%. Removing these brings us below the 15% goal.*

---

## SLIDE 3: Why Outbound Spikes? (Jaydeep's Workgroup Reality)
*   **PIN-Free Access:** Any agent can dial out at any time.
*   **MLT Confusion:** Agents using "waiting for MLT" as an excuse to clear a call when it wasn't required.
*   **Process Leakage:** **Limited to Jaydeep's workgroup**, we identified that end-of-shift volume spiked by 300% due to "AHT Parking."

---

## SLIDE 4: Solution Path & SIG Escalation
*   **System Lock:** Satyam IDM workstation lockdown.
*   **Audit Trail:** Quality Control compliance checks.
*   **SIG Model:** Tickets unresolved after 2 outbound attempts MUST be escalated to the Service Intervention Group (SIG) with associated performance incentives.
*   **People:** Training and engagement managed by **Human Resources (HR)** to reduce attrition-related process gaps.
